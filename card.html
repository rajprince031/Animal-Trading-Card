Tiger

The tiger's stripes serve as excellent camouflage, allowing it to blend into its surroundings while hunting.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris
  • Average Length: 2.5 to 3.3 meters (8 to 11 feet)
  • Average Lifespan: 10 to 15 years (in the wild)
  • Habitat: Tropical forests, grasslands, and savannas

Tigers are the largest species of the cat family and are known for their distinctive orange coat with black stripes. Found primarily in Asia, they are apex predators, primarily hunting large ungulates. Tigers are solitary animals, often marking their territory with scent markings and vocalizations.
